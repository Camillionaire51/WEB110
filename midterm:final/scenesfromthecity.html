New York City has played a starring role in countless films, capturing the chaos, charm, and contradictions of urban life. From gritty 70s thrillers to heartwarming rom-coms, the city has been both a backdrop and a character in its own right. Whether you're a movie buff or just a casual fan, here are some of the most iconic scenes ever shot in the Big Apple.

Breakfast at Tiffany's (1961)

  • Famous Scene: Holly Golightly eating a croissant while gazing into Tiffany's window on Fifth Avenue.
  • Filming Location: Tiffany & Co., 727 5th Avenue, Manhattan
  • Fun Fact: The scene was shot at dawn to avoid crowds, and the croissant Holly eats was a last-minute replacement for a Danish pastry.

Ghostbusters (1984)

  • Famous Scene: The Stay Puft Marshmallow Man attacks the city.
  • Filming Location: 55 Central Park West, Manhattan (the building used to be Dana Barrett's apartment).
  • Fun Fact: The building became a popular tourist stop, known among fans as "Spook Central."

When Harry Met Sally (1989)

  • Famous Scene: The "I'll have what she's having" moment at Katz's Delicatessen.
  • Filming Location: Katz's Delicatessen, 205 E Houston St, Manhattan
  • Fun Fact: The table where the scene was filmed now has a sign that reads, "Where Harry Met Sally...Hope You Have What She Had!"

Home Alone 2: Lost in New York (1992)

  • Famous Scene: Kevin McCallister exploring the Plaza Hotel and Central Park.
  • Filming Location: The Plaza Hotel, 768 5th Ave, Manhattan
  • Fun Fact: The hotel still offers a "Home Alone 2" experience package for their guests.

Spider-Man (2002)

  • Famous Scene: The upside-down kiss between Spider-Man and Mary Jane in the rain.
  • Filming Location: 123 Fifth Avenue, Manhattan
  • Fun Fact: Tobey Maguire had to hold his breath during the kiss because the rain kept flowing into his nose.
