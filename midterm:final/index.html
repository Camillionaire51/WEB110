Dear New York,

You move fast,
but I fell for you slowly
block by block, light by light.

You're the buzz of a taxi at midnight,
the smell of bagels before sunrise.
The echo of footsteps in a subway tunnel,
a saxophone solo drifting through the dark.

You never sleep,
and yet somehow, you still dream.
Of stories told in high-rises,
of strangers who become neighbors,
of moments that feel like movie scenes.

I've seen you loud and glittering,
I've seen you quiet and golden.
In all your moods,
you feel like home.

Yours always,
Camille
