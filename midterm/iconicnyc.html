Statue of Liberty

A gift from France in 1886, Lady Liberty has become the ultimate symbol of freedom and welcome. She stands 305 feet tall on Liberty Island, holding a torch that once guided millions of immigrants into the New World.

Central Park

Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux in 1858, Central Park stretches across 843 acres of Manhattan. It's an urban oasis with woodlands, lakes, trails, and even a zooâ€”visited by over 40 million people each year.

Brooklyn Bridge

Completed in 1883, this iconic suspension bridge connects Manhattan and Brooklyn over the East River. Once the longest bridge of its kind, it's a marvel of 19th-century engineering and a favorite walking path for locals and tourists alike.
